# Knuckles' Chaotix

**Knuckles' Chaotix** is a 1995 platform video game developed by **Sega** and released for the **Sega 32X**. It is a spin-off of the *Sonic the Hedgehog* series and focuses on Knuckles the Echidna alongside a new group of characters known as the **Chaotix**.

---

## Overview

Unlike traditional *Sonic* games that emphasize high-speed platforming, *Knuckles' Chaotix* introduces a unique **partner-based gameplay system**. The player controls one character while being physically linked to a partner by an elastic ring, which greatly affects movement, momentum, and strategy.

The game is set on **Carnival Island**, where the villain **Dr. Robotnik** (Eggman) and a mysterious character named **Metal Sonic** seek to exploit a powerful energy source called the **Chaos Rings**.

---

## Gameplay

* Players choose from several playable characters, each with unique abilities.
* Two characters are linked together by an elastic ring, creating physics-based movement.
* Momentum, swinging, and cooperative positioning are key mechanics.
* Levels are more exploratory, focusing less on speed and more on control.

### Playable Characters

* **Knuckles the Echidna** – Can glide and climb walls.
* **Vector the Crocodile** – Strong and heavy, useful for combat.
* **Espio the Chameleon** – Fast and able to cling to walls.
* **Charmy Bee** – Can fly for short periods.
* **Mighty the Armadillo** – Can perform powerful jumps and withstand spikes.

---

## Story

Knuckles is tricked into guarding Carnival Island by **Dr. Robotnik**, who plans to steal the Chaos Rings. With help from the Chaotix detectives, Knuckles works to stop Robotnik and uncover the truth behind Metal Sonic’s presence.

---

## Development and Release

* Developed by **Sega Technical Institute**.
* Released in **1995** exclusively for the Sega 32X.
* One of the few original titles made specifically for the 32X add-on.

---

## Reception

*Knuckles' Chaotix* received mixed reviews. Critics praised its colorful visuals and experimental mechanics but criticized its inconsistent physics and level design. Over time, it has gained a **cult following** due to its originality and connection to later Sonic lore.

---

## Legacy

* Introduced the **Chaotix characters**, who later appeared in *Sonic Heroes* (2003).
* Influenced future Sonic games with cooperative mechanics.
* Remains a notable experimental entry in the Sonic franchise.
